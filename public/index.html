Guidance / 04

From data to responsible evidence

A practical path for small research teams

Clear next steps for learners and teams that cannot afford commercial discovery platforms.

01⌘

Find the right archive

Match your assay to a domain repository, inspect access conditions, and search for comparable Indian studies before collecting new data.

Open the resource catalog →
02◇

Protect people, not just files

Use coded identifiers, minimise geography, document consent and data-use limits, and seek institutional ethics and legal review.

Read the safety guide →
03⌁

Check before analysing

Validate file structure, reference builds, sample metadata, missingness and reproducibility information before statistical modelling.

Run a free check →
04≡

Report limitations honestly

Indian populations are highly diverse. Never treat one cohort, state, caste, language group or broad ancestry label as representative of India.

Explore responsible standards ↗
!

Important boundary

Omics data can identify families and communities.

This platform is for public-resource discovery, education and lightweight technical checks. It is not a clinical interpretation service, an ethics committee, a legal opinion or a secure repository for human genomic data.

01

Do not upload raw identifiable human data.

02

Do not use a QC summary to diagnose or treat anyone.

03

Verify consent, ethics approval and applicable Indian law.

04

Use validated pipelines and expert review for decisions.
